# Requirements Document

## Introduction

The Notifications feature extends AquaSense's existing alert system to deliver real-time
email notifications to farm operators. Delivery uses EmailJS (client-side, free tier) and
integrates with the existing alert evaluation pipeline in `alerts.js`. Users configure
their email notification preferences, and the system dispatches emails whenever a critical
or warning alert is generated.

> **Out of Scope (deferred):** SMS notifications via Twilio and browser push notifications
> via Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) are explicitly deferred to a future development phase.

---

## Glossary

- **Notification_Service**: The frontend subsystem responsible for dispatching email
  notifications.
- **Alert**: A threshold-breach event generated by the existing `alerts.js` evaluation
  pipeline, classified as `critical` or `warning`.
- **EmailJS**: A free-tier client-side email delivery service that sends email without a
  dedicated mail server.
- **Notification_Preferences**: Per-user settings stored in Firestore that record whether
  the email channel is enabled and the configured email address.
- **User**: An authenticated AquaSense user with a role of `admin`, `owner`, or `farmer`.
- **Cooldown**: A minimum interval between repeated notifications for the same sensor and
  pond, preventing notification spam.

---

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Notification Preference Management

**User Story:** As a farm operator, I want to configure whether email notifications are
active for my account, so that I only receive alerts through the channel I prefer.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Notification_Service SHALL store Notification_Preferences for each User in the
   Firestore `users/{uid}/notificationPrefs` document.
2. WHEN a User enables or disables the email notification channel, THE Notification_Service
   SHALL persist the change to Firestore within 2 seconds.
3. THE Notification_Service SHALL support one independently toggleable channel: `email`.
4. WHEN a User enables the `email` channel, THE Notification_Service SHALL require a
   valid email address before saving the preference.
5. THE Notification_Service SHALL pre-populate the email field with the User's existing
   Firebase Auth email address when the preferences UI is first opened.

---

### Requirement 2: Email Notifications via EmailJS

**User Story:** As a farm operator, I want to receive email alerts when water quality
parameters breach thresholds, so that I can act even when I am away from the dashboard.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN an Alert is generated and the User has `email` enabled in Notification_Preferences,
   THE Notification_Service SHALL send an email to the User's configured address within
   10 seconds of the Alert being created.
2. THE Notification_Service SHALL include the following fields in every alert email:
   pond name, sensor parameter, measured value, severity level, threshold range, and
   timestamp.
3. WHEN the EmailJS API returns an error, THE Notification_Service SHALL log the error to
   the browser console and display a non-blocking toast message to the User.
4. THE Notification_Service SHALL enforce a per-sensor Cooldown of 15 minutes for email
   notifications to avoid exceeding the EmailJS free-tier monthly limit of 200 emails.
5. WHERE the EmailJS service template ID and public key are not configured, THE
   Notification_Service SHALL disable the `email` channel toggle and display a
   configuration warning.

---

### Requirement 5: Notification Dispatch Integration with Alert Pipeline

**User Story:** As a farm operator, I want email notifications to be sent automatically
when the existing alert system detects a threshold breach, so that I do not need to
manually trigger them.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN the existing `sensor-data-updated` event produces a new Alert in `alerts.js`,
   THE Notification_Service SHALL evaluate the User's Notification_Preferences and
   dispatch an email notification if the `email` channel is enabled.
2. WHEN multiple sensors breach thresholds simultaneously, THE Notification_Service SHALL
   dispatch one email notification per breaching sensor, subject to per-sensor Cooldowns.
3. THE Notification_Service SHALL not dispatch notifications for Alerts that have already
   been marked as resolved.
4. WHILE the User's browser is offline, THE Notification_Service SHALL queue email
   dispatch requests and retry them when connectivity is restored, up to 3 retry attempts
   with a 60-second interval between attempts.

---

### Requirement 6: Notification History and Audit Log

**User Story:** As a farm owner, I want to see a log of all email notifications that were
sent, so that I can audit response times and verify that alerts were delivered.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Notification_Service SHALL persist a record of every dispatched email notification
   to the Firestore collection `notificationLog` with the following fields: `uid`,
   `channel`, `alertId`, `pondName`, `parameter`, `severity`, `sentAt`, and `status`
   (`sent` | `failed`).
2. WHEN an email notification dispatch fails, THE Notification_Service SHALL update the
   log record `status` field to `failed` and store the error message in an `errorDetail`
   field.
3. THE Notification_Service SHALL display the 50 most recent notification log entries in
   the Alerts UI, filterable by status.
4. THE Notification_Service SHALL retain notification log records for 30 days, after
   which they SHALL be eligible for deletion by a scheduled cleanup process.
